         DYSPHASIA

Dysphasia is disorder of language as a result of brain injury that affects the ability to communicate, both verbal and written to express ideas and to understand the speech of others. It affects a childâ€™s ability to express ideas in words. All aspects of language; phonic sounds and syllables, words and meanings, speeches and thoughts, writing and narrating, reading and comprehension, grammar and vocabulary becomes difficult to understand. A child struggles to use the right word when trying to communicate and barely grasps meaning from what others say. It is traumatic to be in such a condition. The child may often have loss of attention and trouble following an instruction or direction therefore appears to be behind in the class. This impacts the child social life and behaviour as well.


Parents can visit a speech pathologist to conduct language assessment so as so identify specific problems and devise ways of assisting the child get through the condition. A one to one speech and language therapy by special schools for language disabilities can also help or be considered with the intervention
