         DYSPRAXIA

Dyspraxia also known as Developmental Coordination Disorder (DCD), is a complex neurological condition affecting fine and/or gross motor skills, motor planning and coordination in children and adults. It affects physical coordination and movement which result in problems with language, perception and thought. A child with dyspraxia finds it difficult to think on what to do, and how to do it as related to physical activities using the muscles such as writing, drawing, speaking, dancing, throwing, catching, dressing, knotting, knitting, painting, skipping, sporting, climbing, cycling, etc. The brain has problem transmitting impulses with right judgement. Dyspraxia is not related to intelligence because it is not an intellectual disorder, but can sometimes affect cognitive skills, extend time spent to carry out a task and impact everyday life. Common signs of dyspraxia are bad posture, poor balance, clumsiness, poor eye coordination, exhaustion, unclear speech etc. Children with dyspraxia also struggle with self care, organising their stuffs, mental arithmetic and learning new skills.


There is currently no cure for dyspraxia, nevertheless it can be successfully managed. With the right therapies, there can be improvements in organizational and motor skills for attaining full potentials.
