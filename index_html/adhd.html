         ATTENTION DEFICIT HYPERACTIVITY DISORDER - ADHD

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) â€“ ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder that affects learning, concentration and behaviour. A child with this condition has trouble settling down and will constantly lose concentration focusing and completing a task. The child being easily distracted, will often forget and lose track of what he/she was doing and may eventually abandon his work unbothered.


This disorder often triggers hyperactivity and impulsive behaviours which affects the overall learning capabilities of the child, that is, reading, writing, speaking, listening and math. The child rushes to do things without thinking if it was right or safe or careless and acts in disruptive ways. Sometimes, might be very impatient, Ignoring instructions and disregarding permission request.


Treatment of ADHD may include a combination of therapy, counselling, behavioural and remedial Education training. Treatment can vary depending on the how complicated the symptoms, personality and health condition of the child is but most children learn to manage with the condition and go on to lead normal lives as they grow up.


It has been discovered that other conditions like anxiety disorder, specific learning disability, or autism may co-exist with ADHD. Therefore, careful and extensive medical and educational assessment are essential to eliminate the chances of other conditions existing. If any other problem exists, it is then necessary to evaluate and treat the disorders along with the treatment for ADHD.
