         DYSLEXIA

Dyslexia is a specific learning difficulty that affects learning and comprehending words which also include trouble with acquiring fluency and accurate reading and spelling skills. A child who suffers from dyslexia finds it difficult to read and understand what was read due to confusing recognition and manipulation of phonic sounds, letters, syllables and words in the brain. So, the child is unable to grasp the meaning of words, phrases and paragraphs in a book. Similarly, it is the difficult for the child to tell rhyming, sequencing and ordering of sounds. These generally affect vocabulary skills and reading speed. Most times, the child struggles to remember things and will have hard time following directions and instructions that others in the same age group will do quickly and conveniently. The pace of response is usually slow that even another child much younger will respond before the dyslexic child does regarding reading. However, this is not the matter of intelligence when dealing with other things. Approximately 10% of the population of children are affected with this condition and it may vary from one individual to another in severity. While some have only dyslexia, some may exhibit signs and symptoms of other learning disabilities which make it worse. Some notable signs of dyslexia in a child include: avoidance to read, tension when called to read, low tune of reading, enormous efforts while reading, emotional outburst and frustration after reading. Children as such are often shy to express themselves even if they know what to say because they feel ashamed.
